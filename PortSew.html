I am a mostly self-taught "sewist" since about 2010. Growing up, I was taught how to hand-stitch, cross-stitch and very basic sewing machine skills. In 2010, when I was pregnant with my first baby, I got the bug. The sewing bug. I sewed all of the crib bedding for our new baby including play-yard sheets and blankets. 2010 is where sewing started to snowball into something I really enjoyed and found useful. I love to create useful things with my hands, so sewing was a natural destination.

I started working with woven fabrics making household items, baby items and bags. Now, however, I much prefer to work with knit fabrics (think your stretchy T-shirt) making garments. I make some of my own clothing and items for my kids, as well. I ran a sewing business for about 2 years named Mindful Mabel. I took mostly custom orders for anything from your basic bedding and quilts to whatever my customers could dream up. I eventually chose to close the business to allow myself to create on my own time and on my own projects. It's funny how sometimes getting paid to do something you love can suck all of the joy out. I sew for only personal enjoyment and use now and I'm much happier in this place.
